SECTION 1: WORKING HOURS AND FLEXIBILITY

Standard working hours are 9am–5:30pm Monday to Friday. We operate a flexible working policy — consultants may adjust their start and end times within a 7am–7pm window provided client commitments and team collaboration are not affected. Flexibility is a privilege, not a right, and may be adjusted if it impacts work quality or client service.

Remote working: Consultants may work remotely up to 3 days per week when not required on client site. On-site client days are not negotiable — if a client expects you on site, you must be on site. When working remotely, you must be reachable during standard working hours and must attend all scheduled meetings via video.

Overtime: Consulting is a demanding profession and longer hours are sometimes necessary around key deadlines. However, chronic overworking is not expected or celebrated. If you are consistently working more than 50 hours per week, speak with your line manager.

SECTION 2: EXPENSES POLICY

All business expenses must be pre-approved by your engagement manager before being incurred, except for the following which are pre-approved up to the stated limits:

- Meals (when travelling or working late past 7pm): up to £30 per meal
- Ground transport (taxis, trains): reasonable cost, economy class
- Accommodation (when travelling overnight for client work): up to £180 per night in London, £130 outside London
- Client entertainment: must always be pre-approved, regardless of amount

Air travel: Economy class for all flights under 4 hours. Business class requires Director-level approval and is only permitted for flights over 6 hours.

Receipts: A receipt is required for all expenses over £10. Expenses submitted without receipts will not be reimbursed.

Submission deadline: Expenses must be submitted within 30 days of being incurred. Expenses submitted after 30 days will not be reimbursed without exceptional circumstances approved by the Finance Director.

Personal expenses: Personal expenses must never be submitted on a business expense claim. If a personal expense is accidentally included, notify Finance immediately.

SECTION 3: LEAVE POLICY

Annual leave: All full-time employees receive 25 days of annual leave plus public holidays. Leave must be approved by your line manager at least 2 weeks in advance for absences of up to 3 days, and at least 4 weeks in advance for absences of 4 days or more.

Sick leave: If you are unwell and unable to work, notify your line manager and any affected clients as early as possible on the first day of absence. For absences of more than 3 consecutive days, a doctor's certificate is required. Sick leave up to 10 days per year is paid in full.

Parental leave: We offer enhanced parental leave above the statutory minimum. Primary caregivers receive 26 weeks at full pay followed by 13 weeks at statutory pay. Secondary caregivers receive 4 weeks at full pay. Speak with HR for details.

SECTION 4: PERFORMANCE REVIEWS

Performance reviews are conducted twice per year — in June and December. The review process involves self-assessment, 360-degree feedback from colleagues and clients, and a structured conversation with your line manager.

Performance is assessed against four dimensions:
1. Client impact: The quality of your work and the value delivered to clients.
2. Commercial contribution: Your contribution to business development and revenue.
3. People and culture: How you contribute to the team, develop others, and embody our values.
4. Knowledge and capability: Your technical expertise and investment in your own development.

Promotion decisions are made annually in January, following the December review cycle.

SECTION 5: CODE OF CONDUCT

We expect all employees to behave with integrity, professionalism, and respect at all times — with clients, colleagues, and anyone else they interact with in a professional capacity.

Conflicts of interest: Declare any personal or financial interest that could conflict with your professional duties. This includes personal relationships with clients, financial interests in client organisations, and outside employment. Declarations must be made to your line manager and HR.

Confidentiality: You must not share confidential client information with anyone outside the firm, including former colleagues. This obligation continues after you leave the firm.

Social media: Do not post anything on social media that could embarrass the firm or breach client confidentiality. Do not identify clients by name in social media posts without their explicit written consent.

Gifts and hospitality: Gifts from clients or suppliers with a value over £50 must be declared to your line manager. Gifts over £100 must be declined or donated to charity. Never accept cash gifts.

Reporting concerns: If you witness behaviour that you believe is unethical, illegal, or in breach of this code, report it to your line manager, HR, or via the anonymous whistleblowing hotline (details on the internal portal).
