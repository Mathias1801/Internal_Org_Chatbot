SECTION 1: OVERVIEW

Our project delivery methodology is built around four core phases: Discovery, Design, Delivery, and Close. Every client engagement, regardless of size or complexity, must follow this structure. Deviations require written approval from a Principal or above.

The methodology ensures consistency in how we deliver value to clients, manage risk, and maintain quality standards across all engagements.

---

SECTION 2: PHASE 1 — DISCOVERY

Duration: Typically 2–4 weeks depending on engagement size.

The Discovery phase begins immediately after the contract is signed. The lead consultant is responsible for scheduling a kickoff meeting with the client within 5 business days of contract signature.

Key activities in Discovery:
- Stakeholder mapping: Identify all relevant client stakeholders, their roles, and their influence on the project outcome.
- Current state assessment: Document the client's existing processes, systems, and pain points through interviews and workshops.
- Data collection: Gather quantitative data (KPIs, financials, operational metrics) to establish a baseline.
- Problem framing: Align with the client on a precise problem statement before proceeding to Design.

Deliverables:
- Stakeholder map
- Current state report
- Agreed problem statement (signed off by client sponsor)

A Discovery readout must be presented to the client sponsor before moving to the Design phase. No engagement may proceed to Design without a signed-off problem statement.

---

SECTION 3: PHASE 2 — DESIGN

Duration: Typically 3–6 weeks.

The Design phase involves developing solution options and recommending a preferred approach. Consultants should develop a minimum of two solution options to present to the client — this demonstrates rigor and avoids the appearance of a predetermined answer.

Key activities in Design:
- Hypothesis development: Formulate hypotheses about potential solutions based on Discovery findings.
- Options analysis: Evaluate each option against criteria including cost, feasibility, risk, and expected impact.
- Business case development: Quantify the expected return on investment for the recommended solution.
- Implementation planning: Develop a high-level roadmap for the recommended solution.

Deliverables:
- Options analysis document
- Recommended solution with business case
- High-level implementation roadmap

All Design deliverables must pass an internal Peer Review before being shared with the client. See the Quality Assurance policy for Peer Review requirements.

---

SECTION 4: PHASE 3 — DELIVERY

Duration: Varies by engagement. Typically 8–24 weeks.

The Delivery phase is where the recommended solution is implemented. The lead consultant transitions into a project management role, coordinating workstreams, managing client relationships, and tracking progress against milestones.

Key activities in Delivery:
- Workstream management: Break the implementation into parallel workstreams with clear owners and deadlines.
- Weekly status reporting: Send a written status report to the client sponsor every Friday by 4pm. The report must include: progress against milestones, risks and issues, decisions required from the client, and next steps.
- Change management: Any scope change must be documented in a Change Request form and approved by both the client sponsor and an internal Principal before work proceeds.
- Risk management: Maintain a live risk register updated at least weekly. Escalate any red-rated risks to a Principal immediately.

Deliverables:
- Weekly status reports
- Updated risk register
- Change request log
- Implementation outputs (varies by engagement)

---

SECTION 5: PHASE 4 — CLOSE

Duration: Typically 2–3 weeks.

The Close phase ensures the engagement ends cleanly, learnings are captured, and the client relationship is positioned for future work.

Key activities in Close:
- Final deliverable sign-off: Obtain written sign-off from the client sponsor on all final deliverables.
- Lessons learned session: Conduct an internal retrospective with the full project team within 5 business days of project end.
- Knowledge capture: Submit a project summary to the Knowledge Management system within 10 business days of project end. See the Knowledge Management policy for required fields.
- Client satisfaction survey: Send the standard client satisfaction survey within 2 business days of final sign-off.
- Commercial close: Confirm all invoices are issued and outstanding payments are followed up by the Finance team.

A project may not be marked as Closed in the project management system until all of the above activities are complete.
